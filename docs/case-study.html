The Problem

Before: Four Tools That Didn't Talk

Every part of the business ran on a different, disconnected tool — each one manual, and none of them aware the others existed.

Sheet1 · A1

Leads

Tracked in an Excel sheet — no pipeline stages, no reminders, no priority.

Sheet1 · A2

Hotel Emails

Written from scratch every time — or drafted ad hoc by pasting details into ChatGPT.

Sheet1 · A3

Itineraries

Typed by hand or built as a PDF, trip by trip, with no template to reuse.

Sheet1 · A4

Contracts & Rates

Held in spreadsheets that the sales team quoted from directly — net cost and client price, same sheet.

None of these tools talked to each other. A lead's status lived in someone's memory. Every email and itinerary was rebuilt from zero. And because sales quoted straight from the sheet holding the agency's real hotel cost, there was no wall between what a hotel charged and what a client was quoted — a quiet margin risk as bookings grew across four countries.

What I Built
One platform, two sides — a live sales tool for quoting honeymoon trips on client calls, and an operations system for leads, contracting, reservations, and itineraries, with AI reading contracts and emails under human review at every step.
How It Works

After: One Route, Seven Stops

The same journey a booking actually takes, end to end — each stop replacing one of the manual tools above.

01
Lead Capture

An inquiry becomes a tracked pipeline entry

Logged with a source, a priority flag, and a stage — New → Contacted → Replied → Meeting → Proposal Sent → Booked — instead of a spreadsheet row.

02
Live Quote

Sales quotes on the call, not after it

Hotels and tours are browsed live on screen share; a PIN-locked portal applies the season price and markup automatically — the real hotel cost never appears.

03
Booking

Season pricing calculates itself

Operations selects the hotel and dates; Low, High, or Peak season is detected automatically and the surcharge applied — no manual season calendar.

04
Hotel Email

One click, not a blank page

Availability requests, reminders, and confirmations generate pre-filled with the right dates and rate, and log themselves against the booking automatically.

05
Contracting

New hotels, found without leaving the app

Prospects are found on Google Maps inside the platform and tracked through a signing pipeline — Prospect → Contacted → Negotiating → Signed.

06
Contract Reading

AI reads the PDF, a person checks it

A signed contract is uploaded and AI extracts every room type and season rate for review — instead of retyping numbers by hand.

07
Itinerary

A trip plan becomes a WhatsApp message

Day-by-day activities, transfers, and airport runs are built once and turned into a ready-to-send itinerary message with a single click.

The System

Module by Module — Who Uses What, and Why

The journey above is the summary. Underneath it, nine modules split cleanly across two teams — each one built to replace one specific manual habit.

Used by — Sales Team · 2 modules

Trip Explorer

Browses Bali destinations, hotels, and tours live with the client, screen-shared on the call, building a shortlist together in real time.

→ Replaced switching between PDFs, spreadsheets, and partner sites mid-call.

Sales Portal

A PIN-locked quoting tool, filterable by destination, area, and budget, that always shows the current, correctly marked-up rate.

→ Sales quotes independently, without ever seeing the agency's real hotel cost.
Used by — Operations · 7 modules

Leads / CRM

Every inquiry moves through a pipeline — New → Contacted → Replied → Meeting → Proposal → Booked — with a priority flag and one-click WhatsApp follow-up.

→ Replaced an Excel sheet with no reminders — nothing sits untouched unnoticed.

Reservations

One record per hotel booking, grouped into client folders, with season-aware pricing and one-click hotel emails.

→ The daily hub — replaced tracking confirmations across Gmail threads.

Tours

Builds day-by-day itineraries — transfers, airport runs, activities — into a formatted WhatsApp message with one click.

→ Cut a 10–15 minute manual write-up down to a single click.

Discovery

Searches Google Maps for new hotels in a destination and saves the strongest prospects for outreach.

→ Replaced manually googling hotels one by one to find new partners.

Outreach

Tracks each prospective hotel through a contracting pipeline — Prospect → Contacted → Negotiating → Signed — with ready-made email templates.

→ One view of every hotel relationship, instead of scattered inbox threads.

Rates

The living rate sheet — every contracted hotel's room types and season pricing in one place, feeding Reservations and the Sales Portal automatically.

→ Replaced the spreadsheet sales used to quote directly from.

Inbox (AI)

Paste in a forwarded booking confirmation and AI extracts the client, hotel, dates, and rate — flagging anything that looks missing.

→ Cuts manual re-typing of supplier emails into the system.

What I Learned

The tempting version of this system would let AI-read contracts or AI-drafted emails go straight out the door. But real hotel relationships and real client money were on the line — one misread rate on a PDF, and the mistake reaches an inbox before anyone checks it. So every AI-touching part of the system stops and waits for a human to review before it saves or sends anything. The hard part of adding AI to a live business wasn't making it work. It was designing around the moments it might be wrong.
